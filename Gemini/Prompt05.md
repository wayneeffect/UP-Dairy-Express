By transforming Rodney’s existing commercial route into an automated subscription service, he can turn his everyday windshield time across the mid-to-eastern Upper Peninsula into a highly profitable, streamlined operation.

Because Rodney is already physically driving the truck to regional stores, his logistics costs for adding home and office drops are virtually zero. Every residential or office delivery fee and retail markup he collects is almost pure incremental profit.

Based on localized pricing, distributor margins, and regional density, here is a breakdown of how much money Rodney could realistically make.

## The Revenue Breakdown

Assuming Rodney targets a realistic, manageable phase-one goal of **100 home subscribers** and **15 local office/small business accounts** across his existing Newberry, Manistique, Engadine, and Naubinway territory, the numbers scale up quickly:

### 1. Residential Home Subscriptions

Families and retirees in the rural UP highly value convenience, especially during long lake-effect winters.

* **Average Weekly Order Value:** $35 *(e.g., two gallons of premium/organic fluid milk, a roll of butter, or a tub of fresh cheese curds)*.
* **Delivery Fee:** $5 per drop.
* **Total Weekly Revenue per Home:** $40
* **Rodney's Gross Margin:** ~45% *(High because he buys at wholesale/distributor pricing and incurs no extra vehicle or fuel capital)*.
* **Weekly Net Profit per House:** ~$18
* **With 100 Homes:** **~$1,800 per week ($93,600 per year)**

### 2. Office & Small Business Subscriptions

Local banks, insurance agencies, real estate offices, and motels maintain coffee stations and breakrooms. Right now, employees are likely running to the local grocery store to stock up.

* **Average Weekly Order Value:** $50 *(Multiple gallons, half-and-half, heavy creams, plus premium local coffee pairings)*.
* **Delivery Fee:** $5 per drop.
* **Total Weekly Revenue per Office:** $55
* **Weekly Net Profit per Office:** ~$25
* **With 15 Offices:** **~$375 per week ($19,500 per year)**

## Total Projected Earnings

By layering these subscription drops directly onto his existing commercial store route, Rodney could realistically bring in an extra **$2,175 per week**.

> Over a 52-week year, that translates to **$113,100 in extra net profit** directly into his pocket, using the truck, fuel, and routes he is already paying for.

## How the Automation Protects His Time

To make sure this doesn't add a mountain of paperwork or disrupt his strict commercial store delivery schedule, the website handles 100% of the administration:

* **No Last-Minute Ordering Chaos:** The website enforces a strict 48-hour order lock and automated card charging. Rodney knows exactly how many extra crates to pull from the wholesale dock before he hits the road.
* **Automated Route Injections:** Software like Shopify paired with local delivery apps automatically maps his residential and office stops smoothly in between his major retail grocery store accounts based on ZIP code.
* **The 45-Second Porch Drop:** The website explicitly states the "Porch Cooler Rule." Customers must leave an insulated cooler outside. Rodney pulls up, swaps the milk, grabs any empty glass bottles, and leaves. He doesn't knock or wait, keeping his route fast, efficient, and highly profitable.
